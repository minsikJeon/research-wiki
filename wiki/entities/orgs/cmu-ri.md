# Carnegie Mellon University — Robotics Institute / SCS

## Type

Academic research institution. **User's home institution** (Master of
Science in Robotics program).

## Focus areas relevant to this wiki

Comprehensive across the user's fields: robotics (manipulation, locomotion,
autonomy, aerial), computer vision (3D, video, perception, tracking),
embodied AI, ML foundations, SLAM, NeRF / Gaussian splatting, foundation
models for robotics.

## Notes

CMU now contributes **9 primary sources** to the wiki — up from 6
before this ingest, +3 in one batch — spanning 3D reconstruction,
tracking, streaming perception, 4D motion, and now a substantial
**manipulation cluster** (Track2Act, Dex4D, Pri4R). The user's local
research community is well-represented — particularly so given the
user is in [[shubham-tulsiani]]'s group.

Two distinct CMU research lines now visible:
- **Geometry / 4D reconstruction** (Tulsiani / Scherer / Ramanan
  axis): Point4D, Flow3R, MapAnything, Any4D.
- **Manipulation via point tracks** (Tulsiani × Fragkiadaki axis):
  Track2Act → Dex4D, with [[harley-2022-pips]] / [[zhang-2025-tapip3d]]
  as the upstream TAP infrastructure produced in-house. **The CMU TAP
  line directly feeds the CMU manipulation line.**

Other CMU faculty/groups to watch for as new papers arrive: Jun-Yan
Zhu, Yaser Sheikh, Abhinav Gupta, Deepak Pathak. Add their pages as
their work appears.
