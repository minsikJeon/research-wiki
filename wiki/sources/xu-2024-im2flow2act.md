# Im2Flow2Act

## TL;DR

**Object flow** as a unifying interface between (i) real human videos
and (ii) simulated robot play, enabling real-world manipulation **with
no real robot training data**. Two networks: a *flow generator*
(AnimateDiff-based, trained on cross-embodiment videos) generates a
complete *task flow* from initial frame + language; a
*flow-conditioned policy* (trained purely on sim play data) outputs
actions via a temporal-alignment module + diffusion action head.
Concurrent with [[bharadhwaj-2024-track2act]] — Track2Act uses *full
2D trajectories* with a residual robot-specific policy; Im2Flow2Act
uses *object-only flow* with a fully sim-trained policy.

## Why it matters

Two design choices distinguish Im2Flow2Act from contemporaries:

1. **Object-centric (not grid) flow.** Grid-style flows (ATM,
   [[bharadhwaj-2024-track2act]]) inevitably capture embodiment
   motion — human arm during training, robot arm at deployment — and go
   out-of-distribution. Restricting to object flow avoids this.
2. **Generate the complete task flow once, not next-step.** Avoids the
   visual mismatch between human-occluded frames in training and
   robot-occluded frames at deployment.

Together these let the policy be trained *purely* on task-less
simulated exploration data (random primitive actions on UR5e in PyBullet)
and deployed in the real world with zero real-robot training data.

## Key claims

- **81% average real-world success rate** across 4 tasks
  (pick-and-place, pouring, drawer opening, cloth folding) — rigid,
  articulated, and deformable — with **no real-world robot data** in
  training (Table 2).
- **Object flow beats grid flow** by ~30% on average — grid flow goes
  OOD because human/sphere embodiment present at training is replaced
  by robot arm at deployment.
- **Learned flow→action policy beats heuristic flow→action** (which
  Track2Act uses) for deformable & articulated objects; heuristic is
  good for rigid pick-and-place only.
- **Temporal alignment is necessary** for unstructured exploration
  data — without it the policy can't locate task progress and produces
  unnecessary rotations (real-world Pouring drops 80→0%).
- **Flow into latent (SD VAE) space** makes flow generation tractable
  at higher resolution.

## Methods

### Flow generation network

- Object bounding box from Grounding-DINO → uniform grid sample inside
  → run [[tapir]] on demo videos to get ground-truth rectangular flow
  `F ∈ R^{3×T×H×W}` (u, v, visibility).
- Encode flow with **SD VAE** (frozen encoder; fine-tuned decoder) into
  latent space.
- **AnimateDiff** = inflated SD-UNet with motion modules; LoRA on SD
  weights, motion modules trained from scratch.
- Conditioned on initial RGB frame + task description.

### Flow-conditioned policy (trained in sim only)

1. **State encoder** φ — transformer over N keypoints at current
   frame `f_t` + their initial 3D coords `x_0` (CLS-token summary).
2. **Temporal alignment module** ψ(F_{0:T}, s_t, ρ_t) — predicts the
   latent of the **remaining** task flow `z_t`, supervised by
   `ẑ_t = ξ(f_{t:T'})` (L2 in latent space, ẑ_t detached).
3. **Diffusion action head** — outputs L-step action chunks
   `{a_t,...,a_{t+L}}` (6-DoF end-effector + 1-DoF gripper).

Training data: sim-only UR5e play data generated by predefined random
primitive actions on rigid/articulated/deformable objects. One policy
across all tasks.

## Results

### Simulation (success rate, %)

| Method | Pick&Place | Pouring | Drawer | Cloth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Im2Flow2Act (demo-cond) | 100 | 95 | 95 | 90 |
| Im2Flow2Act (lang-cond) | 90 | 85 | 90 | 35 |
| ATM (Wen et al.) | 50 | 30 | 85 | 30 |
| Heuristic [Track2Act / General-Flow] | 70 | 50 | 30 | 0 |
| GridFlow ablation | 30 | 25 | 35 | 45 |
| No-alignment ablation | 80 | 85 | 90 | 90 |

### Real world (success rate, %)

| Method | Pick&Place | Pouring | Drawer | Cloth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Im2Flow2Act (demo) | 95 | 80 | 90 | 70 |
| Im2Flow2Act (lang) | 90 | 80 | 85 | 70 |
| Heuristic | 70 | 50 | 30 | 0 |

## Limitations / open questions

- **2D flow only** — fails on out-of-plane rotations (screwing) and
  precise z-axis movements (pouring is a frequent fail mode).
  [[zhi-2025-3dflowaction]] addresses this by lifting to 3D flow.
- **Sim-to-real gap on deformables** — cloth folding drops 90→70%
  in real world because deformable sim doesn't match real cloth
  dynamics.
- **One-shot demonstration assumed** for the demo-conditioned setting;
  language-conditioned setting depends on flow generator quality.

## Connections

- **Concurrent with [[bharadhwaj-2024-track2act]]** (Aug 2024 vs.
  Oct 2024) — Track2Act uses *full 2D point trajectories* of all
  scene points + residual *real-robot* policy; Im2Flow2Act uses
  *object-only* flow + fully *sim-trained* policy. Direct ablation:
  Im2Flow2Act compares against the Track2Act heuristic baseline.
- **Direct successor:** [[zhi-2025-3dflowaction]] lifts the same
  AnimateDiff flow generator to 3D and adds GPT-4o-based closed-loop
  verification.
- **Predecessor of Dex4D:** [[kuang-2026-dex4d]] uses the same
  "generated video → 3D point tracks → policy" decomposition but
  ports it to dexterous hands with a learned RL policy.
- **Point tracker dependency:** Uses [[tapir]] (Doersch 2023) to mine
  flow from human demos.
- **Diffusion action head:** Same as [[diffusion-policy]] (Chi 2024).
- **AnimateDiff usage:** Same flow-generation backbone as
  [[zhi-2025-3dflowaction]].
