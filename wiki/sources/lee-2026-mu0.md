# µ0 (mu0)

## TL;DR

**Query-conditioned 3D trace-space world model** for cross-embodiment
manipulation. Instead of predicting pixels (video WM) or actions (VLA),
µ0 predicts **future 3D trajectories** for **semantically selected
interaction points** — objects, tools, hands, contact regions —
represented as **B-spline control points** and trained with
**flow matching**. Data engine **TraceExtract** turns heterogeneous
human + robot videos into event-captioned 3D trace tuples via
(1) DINOv2 entity keypoint sampling, (2) global-local 3D
reconstruction with a shared coordinate frame, (3) motion-centric
chunk captioning. Frozen µ0 pairs with a small **action expert** for
downstream robot control. Beats action-labeled π₀ (30.25% vs 25.25%
avg on RoboCasa365 8 tasks) and matches π₀.₅ despite **zero action
supervision at pretraining**. Real-world UR3 (3 tasks): µ0 91.7% avg
vs π₀.₅ 80% vs TraceGen 81.7% vs π₀ 71.7%. Direct successor of
**TraceGen** (Lee et al. CVPR 2026 — same first author).

## Why it matters

Squarely inside the [[point-tracks-as-manipulation-interface]] family
but shifts the design in three concrete ways vs. its five wiki
siblings:

1. **World model, not just plan generator.** Track2Act/Im2Flow2Act/
   3DFlowAction/Dex4D predict tracks as a *plan* consumed by a
   downstream controller. µ0 frames trace prediction as a
   **query-conditioned dynamics model** ("what will these points do?")
   and reuses its **frozen intermediate features** as motion tokens for
   the action expert. Closer to pixel-space video WMs (Cosmos, VPP,
   Ctrl-World) in intent, but replaces dense appearance with sparse 3D
   traces.
2. **Semantic keypoint sampling replaces fixed grid.** The three
   limitations of prior work µ0 explicitly attacks — under-sampling
   small task-critical regions (tool tips, contact patches), conflating
   object with camera motion, episode-level captions instead of event-
   level — all stem from fixed-grid trace curation. TraceExtract fixes
   all three via DINOv2 entity clusters + globally aligned 3D +
   motion-centric chunk boundaries.
3. **B-spline control points + flow matching.** Unlike Track2Act's DiT
   over dense waypoints or 3DFlowAction's optimization at inference,
   µ0 denoises a **compact spline control set** in a flow-matching
   loop. Adds **validity prediction** (Ldone) and **semantic rigidity
   loss** (Lrig — keypoints within same DINO cluster preserve local
   geometry) on top of flow. Same spline family as
   [[anon-2026-trace-anything]] — but µ0 operates on manipulation
   horizons T∈{8,16,32}, not 200-frame video reconstruction where
   splines break down (per the wiki's own [[trace-anything]] critique).

Bonus signal: µ0 lists **[[kim-2026-pri4r]]** as a related "trace-
based" method in its references (Kim et al. 2603.01549) — confirms
Pri4R sits in the same design cluster from another angle.

## Key claims

### Trace prediction (Table 1) — beats all baselines on 3D, best top5 on 2D

- **3D trace forecasting**: beats TraceGen, Dream2Flow, 3DFlowAction
  on every ADE/FDE/DTW × T ∈ {8,16,32} × top1/top5 metric. µ0 top5-ADE
  T=32 is 0.239 vs TraceGen 0.325 vs 3DFlowAction 0.630.
- **2D trace forecasting**: best top5-ADE/FDE/DTW at every horizon; top1
  competitive with Gemini-3.1-pro, GPT-5.5 (VLM-tokenized baselines) —
  sampled traces contain more accurate goal-directed futures than
  strong VLM zero-shot readers.
- **Inference latency 0.29 s** per prediction — **2.9× faster than
  Track2Act** (0.85 s), **~200× faster than Gemini-3.1-pro API** (78 s),
  **~50× faster than Hamster** (14.4 s).

### Sim RoboCasa365 (Table 2, 8 tasks, avg SR %)

| Method | Category | Avg SR |
|---|---|---|
| Diffusion Policy | scratch | 22.75 |
| π₀ | VLA | 25.25 |
| **π₀.₅** | VLA | **42** |
| TraceGen + AE | video WM | 23 |
| **µ0 + AE** | video WM | **30.25** |

µ0 beats π₀ by **+5.0 pts** despite zero action supervision at
pretraining. Loses to π₀.₅ by **-11.75 pts** but π₀.₅ uses large-
scale action-labeled pretraining (not data-matched).

### Real-world UR3 (Fig 6, 3 tasks, avg SR %)

| Method | Pick&Place | Pour | Unfold | Avg |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| VLM + AE (no trace) | 95 | 80 | 45 | 73.3 |
| π₀ | 90 | 80 | 45 | 71.7 |
| π₀.₅ | 85 | 80 | 75 | 80 |
| TraceGen + AE | 95 | 85 | 65 | 81.7 |
| **µ0 + AE** | 91.7 (avg) | — | — | **91.7** |

µ0 wins overall by +10 pts over TraceGen, +11.7 over π₀.₅, +18.4 over
VLM+AE (same policy arch but no trace expert — this is the clean
ablation showing frozen trace features carry real motion prior beyond
the SmolVLM backbone).

### Scaling (Appendix E.2)

- Trace prediction quality **monotonically improves** with model size
  (best top5-DTW at 2.59B params) and pretraining data.
- **Policy capacity trade-off**: the gap between µ0 + AE and w/o-Trace
  AE **widens as action head shrinks** — trace-space pretraining
  supplies motion structure a small policy can't recover on its own.
  Trace features are load-bearing, not decorative.

### Design ablations (Appendix E.1, Table 6)

Validates every major choice: B-spline > raw waypoints,
DINOv2 > raw pixels, semantic rigidity loss helps, depth input helps
when available, historical traces help.

### TraceExtract scale

**~8×** curation scale over prior 3D trace datasets (TraceGen). Two
paths: (1) event-centric chunks limit VLM context length + align
caption units to sub-goals; (2) global 3D lifting means the same
trace can be observed from arbitrary reference cameras.

## Methods

### TraceExtract data engine (§2)

Three-stage pipeline:

1. **Semantic keypoint sampling (§2.1)**
   - Extract DINOv2 patch features → cluster into entity groups.
   - Propagate entity identity across the clip.
   - Allocate fixed keypoint budget per entity; sample spatially
     diverse points on high-visibility frames.
   - Movement filter marks static/background tracks to suppress non-
     moving points.
2. **3D trace construction (§2.2)** — **global–local reconstruction**
   - Sparse anchor frames establish a shared global coordinate frame.
   - Dense local chunks reconstructed independently, then aligned back.
   - Track keypoints in the common 3D space.
   - Propagate across chunk boundaries using each point's last valid
     world position.
   - Reproject into per-chunk reference camera to get screen-aligned
     3D traces `T_ref,n^{t:t+H} = [x_n,i, y_n,i, z_n,i]_{i=t}^{t+H}`.
     Removes camera motion + preserves image alignment for VLM
     conditioning.
   - **Arc-length reparameterization** normalizes trace speed — smooths
     duration differences between human and robot demos.
3. **Event-centric captioning (§2.3)**
   - Savitzky–Golay smooth per-frame trace acceleration → find action
     anchors as peaks `p_i`; chunk boundaries at low-acceleration valleys.
   - VLM captions each chunk from start / midpoint / end frames.
   - Text-only LLM merges adjacent captions into hierarchical
     (fine + task-level) descriptions.

Output tuple:
```
D_TE = { I_t, l_c, Q_t, T_ref^{t-h:t+H} }
```
where `I_t` = observation, `l_c` = event caption, `Q_t = {q_t^n}` =
query keypoint set, `T_ref^{t-h:t+H}` = past + future 3D traces in
reference camera.

### µ0 architecture (§3)

Prediction map:
```
µ0 : ( I_t, l_c, Q_t, T_ref^{t-h:t} )  →  T̂_ref^{t:t+H}
```

**§3.1 Multi-modal conditioning backbone.**
- Pretrained **SmolVLM2-2.2B** prefix encodes RGB + language.
- **Depth pathway** (optional): trainable patch stem + shared deeper
  SigLIP layers with RGB tokens.
- Trace expert **cross-attends to VLM key-value cache** while
  maintaining a separate motion-specific stream (Shukor et al. 2025
  SmolVLA pattern).

**§3.2 Permutation-equivariant Trace Expert.**
- **Exchangeable queries**: each keypoint is an independent query
  token; all queries share the processing stack; predictions
  permutation-equivariant across keypoint dim.
- **Spline targets**: subtract current 3D anchor, represent future as
  **cubic B-spline control points** (following Liu et al. 2026 —
  Trace Anything).
  - (1) compactness (small control set vs dense waypoints);
  - (2) smoothness (suppresses tracker jitter);
  - (3) easier denoising (lower output dim).
- **Query tokenization** = segment embed (history vs future) + Fourier
  embed of current pixel loc + local DINO features.

**§3.3 Flow matching with semantic structure.**
- Trace expert = conditional flow model over B-spline control points.
- **adaLN-Zero** flow-time modulation.
- Loss:
  ```
  L = L_flow + λ_done L_done + λ_rig L_rig
  ```
  - `L_flow` = control-point velocity field matching.
  - `L_done` = validity prediction (per-step trajectory termination
    under occlusion / track loss).
  - `L_rig` = **semantic rigidity** — keypoints within same DINO
    cluster preserve local geometry.
- Inference = denoising loop → decode control points → reconstruct
  smooth 3D traces.

**§3.4 Trace-conditioned action expert.**
- Freeze pretrained µ0 (VLM backbone + trace expert).
- Train only an **action expert** on top of frozen trace features.
- Policy reads features from a **single partial-denoising step** of µ0
  (not full rollout — no IK replay at every control step).
- Inject via **gated cross-attention** into VLM features.
- Action denoiser conditioned on gripper-camera + proprioception +
  language → continuous action chunks.

Result: video-only WM pretraining transfers to executable robot
policies without action supervision at pretraining time.

### Training details

- Backbone init: SmolVLM2-2.2B.
- Objective: flow matching + validity + rigidity.
- Pretraining data: heterogeneous human + robot manipulation videos
  processed through TraceExtract (~8× TraceGen's dataset).
- Downstream policy: fine-tune action expert only; µ0 frozen.

- Confirms the trace expert (not the VLM backbone) supplies the motion
  prior.

## Limitations / open questions

Authors flag:
- **Inherits perception-stack errors** — bad DINOv2 clustering, bad 3D
  recon, bad tracking, bad captioning all leak into training.
- **No forces / tactile / contact modes** — geometry + motion only.
  Fine manipulation may need more.
- **Tabletop only** — no mobile manip, no dexterous hands, no long-
  horizon composite tasks in evals.

Not flagged by authors but visible from the tables:
- **π₀.₅ still wins in sim** (42% vs 30.25%) — action-labeled
  pretraining is not yet fully replaceable at RoboCasa scale.
- **B-spline control point representation reuses Trace Anything's
  primitive** — the wiki has already flagged Bézier-splines as the
  worst 4D representation at 200-frame horizons. µ0 operates at
  T ≤ 32 (manipulation horizons) where this doesn't bite; scaling
  µ0's traces to long horizons is an open question.
- **Frozen µ0 vs. co-fine-tuned µ0 + action** not compared — the
  choice to freeze is a *modularity* claim, not shown to be optimal.
- **No head-to-head with [[kim-2026-pri4r]]** — Pri4R uses point
  tracks as *privileged supervision* (training-time only, discarded
  at inference); µ0 uses them as an *intermediate representation*
  (frozen features consumed by AE). Different design points but
  overlapping motivations. Direct benchmark comparison would inform
  the axis.

## Connections

- **Direct predecessor:** **TraceGen** (Lee et al. CVPR 2026) — same
  first author. TraceGen predicts 3D traces on a fixed grid with
  episode-level captions and depth-conditioned input. µ0 addresses
  all three of its limitations: fixed grid → semantic keypoints,
  episode captions → event-level, depth-required → depth-optional.
  TraceGen is the primary video-only-pretraining baseline throughout
  the paper. **Not yet in wiki — high-priority next ingest.**
- **Same family (concept page):** [[point-tracks-as-manipulation-interface]].
  See [[cmp-point-track-manipulation]] for head-to-head.
- **Contemporary:** [[kuang-2026-dex4d]] (uses gen video + 3D tracks
  + RL — different: dexterous, no video-only pretraining, plan-vs-
  WM distinction), [[zhi-2025-3dflowaction]] (3D flow + optimization
  policy — µ0's WM framing subsumes this), [[bharadhwaj-2024-track2act]]
  (2D DiT trace denoiser — µ0's 2D top5 beats it).
- **Privileged-supervision counterpart:** [[kim-2026-pri4r]] — cited
  in µ0's refs. Opposite axis: Pri4R discards head at inference; µ0
  consumes frozen features at inference.
- **Backbone:** SmolVLM2-2.2B (Shukor et al. 2025 SmolVLA line) —
  first SmolVLM-family paper in the wiki.
- **Baselines (VLMs):** Gemini-3.1-pro / Gemini-3-flash / GPT-5.5 —
  all beaten on top-5 at ~200× lower latency.
- **B-spline lineage:** Cubic B-spline control points (Liu et al.
  2026 = [[anon-2026-trace-anything]]). µ0 confirms the primitive
  works at manipulation horizons where it fails at long-horizon 4D
  reconstruction.
- **Author cluster:** UMD (Furong Huang lab + Jia-Bin Huang lab) +
  SNU (H. Jin Kim lab). First UMD paper in the wiki; H. Jin Kim
  connects to Korean robotics community (SNU × KAIST × Yonsei — the
  same regional cluster as [[kim-2026-pri4r]]).
- **Adjacent baselines cited but not ingested:** Dream2Flow
  (Dharmarajan et al. 2026 — video gen + 3D object flow), Hamster
  (Li et al. 2025 — hierarchical action model), NovaFlow (Li 2026a),
  RoboCasa365 (Nasiriany 2026 — new benchmark), Physical Intelligence
  π₀ / π₀.₅ (already the primary comparison targets across the wiki's
  VLA line).
